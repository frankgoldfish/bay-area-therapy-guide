Finding the right therapy support shouldn’t feel like a second full-time job.

Bay Area Therapy Guide is a pediatric therapy navigation concierge for parents who need help finding the right next steps in speech, occupational, physical, feeding, or developmental support.

Join the early interest list See how it works

Built for parents in San Francisco, the Peninsula, South Bay, East Bay, and Marin who want a calmer, more organized way to understand their options. We are not a clinic, and we do not provide medical advice — we help families navigate the process with more clarity.

What we help with

  • Making sense of options
    Private clinics, hospital systems, regional center pathways, school-based support, and developmental evaluations can be hard to compare.
  • Reducing waitlist chaos
    We help families identify realistic next steps instead of losing weeks calling providers one by one.
  • Preparing for outreach
    Know which questions to ask, what paperwork matters, and where to start based on your child’s needs.

The problem

Parents often realize they need support and then run straight into long waitlists, scattered information, unclear terminology, and a dozen different intake processes.

What we do

We act like a navigation concierge — helping families understand the landscape, prioritize options, and move forward with more confidence.

Why Bay Area-specific

The provider landscape, geography, commute burden, and mix of school, private, and health-system options here are unusually complex. Local context matters.

How the concierge works

This is a lightweight service concept designed to save families time early in the process.

1

Tell us what’s going on

Share your child’s age, your location, what concerns you’re seeing, and what kind of help you’re looking for.

2

We map the options

We organize likely paths to explore — for example speech therapy, OT, PT, feeding support, developmental evaluation, or regional resources.

3

You get a clearer action plan

We help you understand where to start first, what tradeoffs matter, and what questions to ask when contacting providers.

4

You move faster with less stress

The goal is not more information — it’s less thrash, fewer dead ends, and a more manageable path forward.
